Shushumow

Description:

Shushumow, which also has many other names (kalkals, zinanaande), is one of the delicacies made during holidays and weddings in Somali culture. by MyHalwad

Ingredients:

Dough:
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ⅓ cup canola oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ cup warm water
  • 2 cups canola oil for frying
Simple Syrup:
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • ¼ cup water
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cardamom

Make It

  1. step1: Make dough: Mix together flour, 1/3 cup canola oil, egg, sugar, baking powder, and salt in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment. With the mixer on low speed, add warm water slowly and mix until a soft dough forms, 4 to 5 minutes. Let dough rest for 30 minutes.
  2. step2: Transfer dough to a clean work surface. Cut dough into about thirty 1-inch pieces. Press each piece onto the back of a fork and curl it out away from you to make shushumow.
  3. step3: Heat 2 cups oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan over medium heat. Working in batches, fry shushumow in hot oil, stirring slowly, until golden brown, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain on paper towels.
  4. step4: Make simple syrup: Combine sugar, water, and cardamom in a large saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let simmer until sugar is dissolved, 1 to 2 minutes more. Remove from heat.
  5. step5: Stir shushumow gently into simple syrup until evenly coated. Transfer shushumow to a plate and let cool.
